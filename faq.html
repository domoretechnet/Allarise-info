Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about Allarise.

General

Allarise is currently iOS only. We may consider expanding to Android in the future.

Pricing & Pro

Pro unlocks sleep sounds, the Morning Weather card, wallpaper presets, Home Assistant / MQTT integration, and more. Trial availability is shown in the App Store before purchase.

Pro is currently available as a monthly subscription or a one-time purchase through the App Store. Pricing, trials, and availability may vary by region and are shown in the App Store before purchase.

Open the app, go to Settings, and tap Restore Purchases. Make sure you are signed in to the same Apple ID used for the original purchase.

Alarms

Usually, yes. Allarise is designed to play audio through the iOS media/playback channel, which is separate from the ringer switch. Actual behavior can still depend on your iPhone's Focus, notification, audio, and system settings.

Note: iOS Focus modes (e.g. Do Not Disturb with "Allow from nobody") can still suppress notifications. We recommend allowing Allarise as a notification exception in any Focus modes you use overnight.

Allarise schedules a system-level backup notification alarm so your alarm can still alert you if the app is not active. The backup experience may differ from the full in-app alarm experience, including volume behavior and mission features.

For the full alarm experience (custom volume, missions, MQTT triggers), keep the app running in the background.

Missions are tasks you must complete before the alarm can be dismissed — so you can't just tap snooze half-awake and go back to sleep. Options include math problems, a phone shake challenge, and Home Assistant actions.

Home Assistant & MQTT

No. Home Assistant and MQTT are completely optional. Allarise is designed to work as a standalone alarm app without any smart home setup.

Allarise is designed to work with standard MQTT brokers over TCP or TLS. Most users run the Mosquitto add-on inside Home Assistant. See the Home Assistant setup guide for step-by-step instructions.

Yes. Each phone uses a unique Device Name as part of its MQTT topic, which helps keep devices separated in normal use. Add one integration entry in Home Assistant per device.

Sleep Sounds

Usually, yes. Sleep sounds use the iOS media playback channel and are designed to continue when the screen locks. Lock screen and Control Center playback controls are typically available, subject to iOS behavior.

Yes. When setting up sleep sounds you can choose a stop time — either a fixed time or a duration. The app is designed to fade out and stop playback automatically at the selected time.
